The Lead Story

Teaching machines to read 80,000 documents a day — and getting 99.85% of it right.

Being a brief account of the work of one AI engineer, in four acts.

Amit Kumar Singh builds the kind of AI systems that don't get demos — they get uptime. At Real Brokerage he leads an agentic document-intelligence platform that reads dense, inconsistent real-estate paperwork with 5+ specialized models fused in a single pipeline: object detection, OCR, vision-language models, reasoning LLMs, and embedding models, coordinated by agents with tool-calling and subagents.

The models themselves are not off-the-shelf. Open weights — LLaMA 3.3, Qwen 3, Gemma — are adapted with LoRA and QLoRA, then post-trained with GRPO reinforcement learning to reason about the domain; hosted frontier models (Gemini 3.5 Pro/Flash, GPT-4o) are tuned to work alongside them.

The result: extraction accuracy rose from 35% to 99.85%, and the platform now processes 80,000+ PDFs every day as microservices on Kubernetes across AWS and GCP.

His earlier career was spent in production computer vision — real-time sports analytics at 3× frame rate, satellite segmentation with 90% of post-processing eliminated — which is where the inference-optimization instincts come from: quantization, TensorRT, graph fusion, and when necessary, surgery on the architecture itself.

The best model is the one that ships — and keeps shipping at 4 a.m.

The Systems

I.

The Agentic Document-Intelligence Platform

Real Brokerage — in production, 80K+ PDFs a day

Manual review of real-estate documents couldn't scale. The answer was decomposition: cooperating agents over 5+ models — detection, OCR, vision VLMs, reasoning LLMs, embeddings — with open models fine-tuned via LoRA/QLoRA and GRPO RL post-training. Deployed as microservices on Kubernetes across AWS & GCP.

Accuracy 35% → 99.85%.

VLM · YOLOv9 · Doctr OCR · GRPO · Kubernetes
II.

One Router to Rule the Models

Multi-LLM orchestration infrastructure

Every team was hand-rolling LLM calls. A central intent-routing layer over Gemini 3.5 Pro/Flash, GPT-4o, LLaMA 3.3, Qwen 3, and Gemma — with per-task LoRA fine-tunes and graceful fallback — became the organization's default path to automation, saving thousands of developer-hours weekly.

Intent routing · RAG · Structured output · Vertex AI
III.

Retrieval, Done Precisely

End-to-end RAG pipeline

Naive RAG retrieves plausible-but-wrong context. This one routes intent, chunks semantically, searches dense and keyword indexes, reranks, and fuses multi-source evidence before the LLM ever reasons — the retrieval backbone behind the platform's daily volume.

Hybrid retrieval · Reranking · Embeddings · BM25
IV.

The Agent That Reviews the Code

Claude Agent SDK automation

Triggered by PRs, incidents, and webhooks, an agentic loop reads diffs, runs tests, inspects logs, spawns subagents, and posts findings — returning senior-engineer hours to feature work.

Tool-calling · Subagents · Webhooks
V.

Sport, at the Speed of Sport

Real-time video analytics — Streamingo AI

Detection, multi-object tracking, pose estimation, and court localization — Swin/VideoMAE/ViT backbones compiled into TensorRT engines with INT8/FP16 quantization and batched streams: 3× the frame rate on identical GPUs.

TensorRT · ONNX · Pose · Tracking
VI.

Maps, Redrawn by Machine

Satellite segmentation — Attentive AI

HRNet and Vision Transformer networks segmenting property features from orbit — +12% accuracy and 90% of post-processing eliminated, with end-to-end ownership of two products.

HRNet · ViT · Geospatial
